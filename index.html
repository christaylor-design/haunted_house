🎧 Haunted House Audio Designer

Build a haunted attraction soundscape and learn how game audio layers shape the player experience.

🎵 Music

Music sets pace, tone and tension. It can make a scene feel calm, urgent, mysterious or threatening.

🌫 Ambient sound

Ambience creates the world around the player: room tone, wind, machines, distant noises and atmosphere.

💥 Sound effects

SFX give feedback for actions and events. In this game, SFX can trigger visual events once the player purchases an event trigger and applies it to one owned SFX. Red lights, flashing lights and ghost events can each be used as separate upgrades.

🗣 Dialogue

Dialogue communicates information, character presence and story. Here, dialogue works as a sensor-triggered scare line.

Learning outcomes

  1. Use music, ambience, SFX and dialogue in games design.
  2. Explore how layering different sound types can create a stronger player experience.
  3. Practice making design choices using budget, variety, repetition and player/customer response.

Tip: repeated sounds lose impact as the customer gets used to them. Combine different layers to build a richer design.
